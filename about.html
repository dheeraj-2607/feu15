History

Our club began with a shared visionâ€”to create a space where students from different disciplines could come together, exchange ideas, and turn their creativity into impactful projects. It started as a small group of enthusiastic students from the Electrical and Electronics, Electronics and Communication, and Computer Science departments, brought together by a shared passion for solving real-world problems through innovation.

The initial spark came when a group of friends realized there was a gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application. With the aim of bridging that gap, they worked together on small technical setups and projects, like basic IoT systems and digital IC designs. Their success and camaraderie inspired others to join, turning a simple idea into an organized platform for collaboration.

As our club grew, so did our ambitions. From organizing technical workshops to designing interdisciplinary projects like the F15 Fuego Buggy, our journey reflects our commitment to innovation and teamwork. We are proud to have evolved into a thriving community where students can explore their potential, learn hands-on skills, and contribute to meaningful advancements in technology and engineering.

Who we are

The F15 Fuego Club is a vibrant community of students from diverse engineering backgrounds, including Electrical & Electronics Engineering (EEE), Electronics & Communication Engineering (ECE), and Computer Science Engineering (CSE) of government engineering college Idukki. Our members share a passion for problem-solving, innovation, and hands-on learning. We take pride in the collaborative spirit that drives every project, from brainstorming to implementation. Each member brings unique skills and perspectives to the table, making our projects both exciting and impactful.
